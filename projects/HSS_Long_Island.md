## **Design Notes**

## **HVAC Systems**

The project utilizes custom Haakon air handling units, which are typically premium, high-quality units designed for healthcare applications. The air-source heat pump system consists of seven AERMEC NYK500HD modules, providing both heating and cooling capability. This modular approach offers redundancy and the ability to stage capacity based on load requirements.

## **Power Systems**

The backup power system features a Caterpillar D600 GC diesel generator (600 kW) connected to four ASCO 7000 Series automatic transfer switches. The multiple transfer switches suggest a sophisticated power distribution system with separate emergency power branches, which is common in healthcare facilities to meet code requirements (critical, equipment, and life safety branches).

## **Air Handling Units**

The custom Haakon AHUs are likely specified to meet stringent healthcare requirements, including infection control, noise criteria, and specific airflow/filtration needs. These units are typically constructed with heavy-gauge materials, double-wall construction, and hospital-grade components.

## **Distribution Systems**

The project appears to use a hydronic distribution system with the modular heat pumps providing both chilled and hot water. This approach eliminates the need for separate boiler systems and can offer energy efficiency advantages in the New York climate.

## **BuildVision Recommendations**

## 1. HVAC System Optimization: The seven-module AERMEC heat pump system provides significant redundancy, which is important for a healthcare facility. However, consider if fewer, larger modules might reduce initial costs while still providing adequate redundancy. For example, a 5-module configuration at 175 tons each might provide similar reliability with reduced connection complexity.

## 2. AHU Alternatives: While Haakon provides excellent custom units, consider other manufacturers with healthcare expertise like Daikin Applied, Trane, or York/JCI. Request "healthcare grade" specifications that match Haakon's quality level rather than settling for standard commercial units. Key specifications to match include:
   - Double-wall construction with solid inner liners (no perforations)
   - Hospital-grade drainage pans with proper slope and connections
   - Premium filtration systems (MERV 14 or higher)
   - Low air leakage rates (Class 6 or better)
   - Antimicrobial interior surfaces
   - Premium fan systems with responsive variable speed control

## 3. Generator System: The Caterpillar D600 GC generator is appropriately sized, but we recommend soliciting competitive bids from Kohler, Cummins, and MTU. Request "healthcare grade" specifications, which should include:
   - Extended runtime capability (minimum 72 hours)
   - Enhanced sound attenuation
   - Robust paralleling capabilities if future expansion is anticipated
   - Premium fuel filtration systems
   - Advanced monitoring and control systems compatible with building automation

## 4. Transfer Switch Alternatives: ASCO 7000 Series transfer switches are top-tier equipment. If seeking alternatives, Russelectric offers comparable quality and reliability. Lower-cost manufacturers like Eaton or Kohler should be evaluated carefully to ensure they meet healthcare code requirements and reliability expectations.

## 5. Project Phasing Coordination: For healthcare facility fit-outs, we recommend careful coordination of equipment delivery schedules with construction phasing. The long lead times for custom AHUs and generators (20-30 weeks) should be factored into the project schedule early. Consider:
   - Early release of equipment packages
   - Phased delivery to match construction sequence
   - Temporary conditioning options if permanent equipment cannot be installed in time for commissioning

## 6. Energy Efficiency Opportunities: The AERMEC heat pump system offers good efficiency, but consider specifying enhanced control systems that optimize the operation of all modules. This might include:
   - Advanced BMS integration
   - Predictive load management
   - Heat recovery optimization
   - Smart defrost cycling for winter operation

## 7. System Redundancy: For a healthcare facility, validate that the proposed systems meet the required redundancy levels. This should include:
   - N+1 redundancy for critical cooling/heating systems
   - Appropriate emergency power distribution
   - Backup control systems
   - Failsafe modes for critical areas

## **Conclusion**

Based on our analysis, this project features premium equipment appropriate for a healthcare facility. While the specified manufacturers represent excellent quality, there are multiple viable alternatives that could offer similar performance potentially at lower cost. The custom nature of the air handling units and the modular approach to the heating/cooling system suggest a well-designed system with appropriate redundancy.

The key opportunities for optimization include careful evaluation of the AHU specifications to ensure alternatives truly match the required quality level, potential consolidation of the heat pump modules to reduce complexity, and early coordination of long-lead equipment with the construction schedule. Given the healthcare application, we recommend prioritizing reliability and performance over initial cost savings when evaluating alternatives.
